ShipsTime is a ship time management app built for cruise crew. We believe in simple, honest data practices. This policy explains exactly what data the app uses and why.
Data collection

Ship name

You enter your ship name during onboarding. It is stored locally on your device and used as a search term to retrieve your ship's voyage information, port schedule, and position from a third-party maritime data service. It is not personal data, is not linked to your identity, and is not used for advertising or tracking.

Ship time & alarm settings

Your ship time offset, alarm times, and timer preferences are stored locally on your device only. They never leave your device.

App preferences

Theme, time format, and other settings are stored locally on your device only.

Not collected
  • Your name, email address, or any personal identity information
  • Your device location
  • Device identifiers or advertising IDs
  • Data for analytics, advertising, or tracking purposes
  • Health, financial, or payment information
  • Contacts, messages, or calendar data
Third-party services

Maritime data service

ShipsTime queries a third-party maritime data service using your ship name to retrieve voyage schedules and position information. This is a functional query — equivalent to a search term. No personal information about you is included in this query. We do not control the third-party service's own data practices.

Google Play Billing

If you purchase a Plus or Pro subscription, payment is processed entirely by Google Play. ShipsTime never sees, receives, or stores your payment information. Google's privacy policy governs the transaction.

Storage & retention

All app data is stored locally on your device using Android's standard secure storage. We operate no servers and store nothing remotely. App data remains on your device until you uninstall ShipsTime or clear app data. Because we store nothing remotely, we have nothing to delete on your behalf.

Children

ShipsTime is designed for cruise ship crew — working adults. We do not knowingly collect data from anyone under 18.

Changes

We update this policy when our data practices change. The effective date above always reflects the current version.
